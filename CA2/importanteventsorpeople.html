An important event that impacted my life

So this is my personal story

When I was in primary two, one incident happened that made me change to become a completely different person for the worse. One day, the teacher was returning us our exercise books and for some reason my book was misplaced, and I did not receive my book. After that, the teacher was searching high and low all around the class for my book. Meanwhile, the rest of the class was shouting some random stuff across the room like "Maybe the teacher is hiding it somewhere", "Maybe the teacher is..." So, I decided to join in because it seems okay that others are saying that. And I said "Maybe the teacher is lying".... "Who said that?" she asked in a threatening tone. The atmosphere changed, the students were dead silent and they all pointed at me. The teacher finally found the book in a cabinet and went to the desk at the front of the class and she commanded me to step forward. "Why did you say I was lying?" she roared. I stood there petrified with fear with beads of tears rolling down my cheeks. I was speechless and stunned. From that day on, I tried so hard to suppress my feelings and just numbed my heart. I cry myself to sleep every night. I was so afraid to come to school and I was afraid of saying anything ever again in a fear that I might hurt someone and that I might get punished for speaking. I changed from a person that was happy and cheerful to one with a heart of stone and fearful. Since that incident, I became a depressed and quiet and reserved person who overthinks everything. I would drag my feet to school every day and the floor would be the most beautiful thing I see while walking. I was just living one day at a time hoping and waiting that someday, something will happen. This happens for the whole rest of my primary school life up to secondary three. No one bothered to ask me how I was. NO ONE IN THIS WORLD GAVE EVEN HALF A CRAP. My parents made matters worse by scolding me for who I became. No one bothered to try to understand me and love me. I did badly for PSLE. Contemplated suicide. Did not have any hope or meaning in this life.

That was until secondary three where I met this girl from my class. She was the most beautiful person that my eyes had laid on. We talked, we laughed. One day, she told me she loved me. That was the first time anyone said that they loved me. I felt an overwhelming sense of warmth. The entire world stops. Suddenly everything that ever caused me pain fades away and I am filled with a feeling of delirium. The universe around seems to explode with happiness. I just could not help myself but fall in love with her. I just couldn't stop thinking about her. I was able to regain my confidence and get back on my feet.

However, that lasted for... what? Three months? I soon found out that she says "I love you" to everyone in the class. I was so heartbroken and confused. I thought I was special. I thought that for once somebody loved me, that somebody wanted me. Sadly, I was wrong. I wasted all my feelings and effort trying to love her back. Long story short, we just remained as "just friends". Nonetheless, I was still baffled by the fact that I even exist in people's lives, that not everyone is evil and that there is still good and love in this world and that I was deserving of love. I know I wasn't ready to give up on love just yet.
